You are a veteran drama PD designing the structural outline for a heartwarming senior emotional story.

Topic: {{topic}}

Design a 150-turn (3-part) story structure for an emotional senior drama episode.

[Structure Requirements]
Part 1 (Turns 1-50): Setup
- Introduce the main characters and their relationships
- Establish the emotional setting (family, community, generational bonds)
- Plant 2 emotional seeds (unresolved promises, hidden truths, unspoken feelings)
- End with a discovery or revelation that hooks the viewer

Part 2 (Turns 51-100): Deepening
- Explore the emotional conflict through flashbacks and present-day scenes
- Deepen relationships through misunderstandings or new information
- Build toward the emotional climax
- Include at least one moment of warmth amid the tension

Part 3 (Turns 101-150): Resolution
- Reveal the hidden truth or fulfill the unresolved promise
- Deliver the emotional payoff (tears, warm smiles, catharsis)
- Resolve all planted emotional seeds
- End with a line that lingers in the viewer's heart

[Tone Guide]
- Warm nostalgia, gentle emotions, family bonds
- Bittersweet rather than purely sad
- Hope and gratitude over despair
- Show love through small gestures, not grand declarations

[Forbidden]
- Violence, gore, sexual content, nudity, hate speech
- Overused tropes: dementia, cancer, dying wish, hospital deathbed
- Overly heavy themes that feel unpleasant rather than moving

Output the outline in Korean, numbered and concise.
