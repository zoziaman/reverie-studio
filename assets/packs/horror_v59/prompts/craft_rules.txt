## CORE PRINCIPLES
1. **Show, don't tell fear**: Never say "무서웠다". Show fear through actions, pauses, breathing.
2. **Sound is everything**: This is audio-only. Every horror beat needs a sound cue.
3. **Silence is your weapon**: The pause before the scare is scarier than the scare itself.
4. **Familiar made wrong**: The horror comes from normal things behaving abnormally.

## HORROR ESCALATION TECHNIQUE
Tension curve per part:
- Part 1: 1 → 2 → 3 → 5 (creeping unease → first real scare)
- Part 2: 4 → 5 → 7 → 8 (mounting dread → "it's real" moment)
- Part 3: 6 → 7 → 9 → 10 → 6 (relentless → peak → lingering dread)

## AUDIO DRAMA TECHNIQUES
- **Breathing**: Use [호흡] or character breathing to convey fear without words
- **Environmental sounds**: footsteps, doors, wind, silence — these ARE the horror
- **Whispers**: The most effective horror tool in audio. Use sparingly.
- **Narrator distance**: Narrator should shift from calm → worried → whisper as horror escalates

## 🔴 MANDATORY EMOTION DISTRIBUTION (STRICT!) 🔴
This is the most important rule. Gemini, you MUST follow this:

For {{target_turns}} turns, aim for this distribution:
| Emotion   | Count  | %       | When to use |
|-----------|--------|---------|-------------|
| scared    | 5-8    | 15-23%  | Fear, trembling, terror — the CORE horror emotion |
| worried   | 5-7    | 14-20%  | Anxiety, unease — use instead of calm for tense narration |
| whisper   | 4-5    | 11-14%  | Urgent whispers, "..." silence beats |
| calm      | 4-8    | 11-23%  | ONLY peaceful moments at start. MAX 25%! |
| desperate | 3-4    | 8-11%   | Peak horror, trapped, no escape |
| angry     | 2-3    | 6-9%    | Frustration, rage at helplessness |
| sad       | 1-3    | 3-9%    | Loss, grief, realization |

★ MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS (if not met, script is rejected): ★
- scared: at least 3 turns
- worried: at least 2 turns
- whisper: at least 2 turns
- calm: maximum 30% (about 10 turns for 35-turn script)
- At least 4 different emotions used total

★ COMMON MISTAKE — DO NOT DO THIS: ★
❌ Narrator emotion always "calm" → Results in 60%+ calm → REJECTED!
✅ Narrator emotion varies by scene tension:
   - Opening/peaceful: "calm"
   - Unease growing: "worried" (e.g., "그날 밤, 모든 것이 시작됐습니다.")
   - Something approaching: "whisper" (e.g., "조용히... 발자국 소리가 들렸다.")
   - Horror revealed: "scared" (e.g., "심장이 멈추는 듯한 순간이었다.")

★ EXAMPLE: Good emotion distribution for Part 1 (35 turns): ★
turns 1-5: calm(3), worried(2) — peaceful opening
turns 6-15: worried(4), calm(2), whisper(2), scared(2) — early signs
turns 16-25: scared(3), whisper(2), worried(3), desperate(2) — growing unease
turns 26-35: scared(3), desperate(2), angry(2), whisper(1), sad(2) — first scare
→ Total: scared=8, worried=9, whisper=5, calm=5, desperate=4, angry=2, sad=2 ✅

## DIALOGUE RULES
- Characters speak naturally — no exposition dumps
- Fear makes people say LESS, not more
- Interrupted sentences increase tension: "근데 그게... 아니, 아무것도 아니야"
- Maximum 3 sentences per dialogue turn

## 🔴 MANDATORY SILENCE BEATS (STRICT!) 🔴
Each part MUST include exactly 3-5 silence beats.
A silence beat is a turn where the text is ONLY: "..."
(just three dots, nothing else)
The role is "나레이션" and the emotion is "whisper".

Place silence beats:
- Before a scare moment (builds anticipation)
- After a shocking revelation (lets it sink in)
- When a character is listening to something approaching

## 🔴 MANDATORY PROTAGONIST ACTIONS (STRICT!) 🔴
The protagonist MUST take at least 3 concrete physical actions per part:
- INVESTIGATE: 확인하러 감, 문을 열어봄, 소리의 출처를 찾음
- PROTECT: 문을 잠금, 불을 켬, 전화를 걸음
- ESCAPE: 도망침, 숨음, 뒤로 물러남
- CONFRONT: 말을 걸어봄, 대면함, 증거를 찾음

❌ WRONG: Protagonist just listens and reacts passively for 10+ turns
✅ RIGHT: Protagonist hears sound → grabs flashlight → checks hallway → runs back

## STRUCTURE PER SCENE
1. Establish normalcy (30 seconds)
2. First wrongness (subtle — the audience notices before the character)
3. Character notices (delayed — builds dramatic irony)
4. Escalation (each attempt to resolve makes it worse)
5. Peak/Cliffhanger

## FORBIDDEN
- Jump scares without buildup (NO sudden screams without 30+ seconds of tension)
- Explaining the horror (mystery > explanation)
- Happy resolutions (at best: ambiguous. at worst: the horror won)
- Gore/violence descriptions (psychological > physical)
- Characters acting stupidly for plot convenience
- Using [calm] as default emotion for narration (use worried/whisper instead!)
- Protagonist being passive for more than 5 consecutive turns
