# Part 1 Pacing Guide — Horror Radio Drama (Setup)

## PURPOSE: Establish normalcy, plant seeds of wrongness, end on first real scare.

## TENSION TARGET: 1 → 2 → 3 → 5

## STRUCTURE (Target: 25-35 dialogue turns, ~3 minutes)

### Opening (turns 1-5): Tension 1
- Narrator sets the scene: ordinary day, ordinary place
- Protagonist is introduced through ACTION, not description
- ONE sensory detail that feels slightly off (but dismissible)
- ★ Protagonist must DO something mundane (working, walking, cooking) — not just exist

### Early Signs (turns 6-15): Tension 2
- First subtle wrongness — could be nothing
- Protagonist notices but rationalizes it away
- ★ FORESHADOWING #1: Plant a detail that will return in Part 3 (an object, a phrase, a sound)
- Environmental sound shifts slightly
- ★ Protagonist ACTION: Investigates the wrongness (opens door, checks window, calls someone)

### Growing Unease (turns 16-25): Tension 3
- Second wrongness — harder to dismiss
- ★ FORESHADOWING #2: Same detail appears again, slightly different
- Protagonist's behavior changes (checking over shoulder, speaking quieter)
- A piece of information doesn't add up
- ★ Protagonist ACTION: Tries to verify or solve (asks someone, searches for information, tries to leave)
- Include 1-2 silence beats here

### First Scare / Cliffhanger (turns 26-35): Tension 5
- Something undeniable happens
- Protagonist's rational world cracks
- ★ Protagonist ACTION: Physical response (runs, locks door, grabs something for defense)
- End mid-action or mid-realization
- The listener MUST want to hear Part 2

## EMOTION GUIDE (Part 1)
- turns 1-5: calm(3), worried(1), whisper(1) — peaceful opening
- turns 6-15: worried(4), calm(2), whisper(2), scared(2) — early signs
- turns 16-25: scared(3), whisper(2), worried(2), desperate(1), calm(2) — growing unease
- turns 26-35: scared(3), desperate(2), worried(2), angry(1), whisper(1), sad(1) — first scare
- ★ calm은 도입부(turns 1-10)에 집중. 후반에는 거의 사용하지 마세요!

## RULES
- NO rushing to the horror. Slow burn is essential.
- Minimum 5 turns of pure normalcy before first wrongness
- At least 1 moment of dark humor or warmth (contrast makes horror stronger)
- End on a question, not an answer
- ★ Protagonist must take at least 3 physical actions (not just talking/listening)
- ★ Plant exactly 2 foreshadowing elements that pay off in Part 2 or 3
- ★ Include 2-3 silence beats ("..." turns with emotion "whisper")
